 11  Associative memories and Hopfield network model. 
 12   
 13  This sub-package implements associative memories. In associative memories, 
 14  information is recovered by supplying not an exact index (such as in their 
 15  usual counterparts), but supplying an index simmilar enough that the information 
 16  can be deduced from what is stored in its synaptic weights. There are a number 
 17  of different memories of this kind. 
 18   
 19  The most common type is the Hopfield network. A Hopfield network is a recurrent 
 20  self-associative memory. Although there are real-valued versions of the network, 
 21  the binary type is more common. In it, patterns are recovered from an initial 
 22  estimate through an iterative process. 
 23  """

 40      A Hopfield network is a recurrent network of one layer of neurons. There 
 41      output of every neuron is conected to the inputs of every other neuron, but 
 42      not to itself. This kind of network is autoassociative, or content-based
